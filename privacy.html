Privacy Policy

Last updated: 18 June 2026

KORVA Inspect ("Korva," "we," "us") is a voice-to-report tool used by licensed timber pest and termite inspectors in Australia to record inspection findings and generate compliant inspection reports. This policy explains what personal information the app collects, how it is used, who it is shared with, and how you can contact us about it.

This policy applies to personal information collected through the KORVA Inspect application, including information about property owners, occupants, and clients whose properties are the subject of an inspection ("clients"), as well as information about the inspectors using the app ("technicians").

Korva is currently operated as an individual project, with formal business registration planned for the future.

What information we collect

Depending on how the app is used, we may collect:

  • Client and property information, including names, addresses, and details about the property being inspected, as captured by the technician during an inspection.
  • Voice recordings and transcripts of the technician's spoken observations during an inspection, which may reference the client's name, property address, and details about the condition of the property.
  • Inspection findings, including descriptions of termite or pest activity, damage observations, conducive conditions, and recommendations, as entered or dictated by the technician.
  • Photographs of the property, where the photo capture feature is in use, which may incidentally include identifiable people, vehicles, or other personal property.
  • Signatures, captured digitally from the technician and, where applicable, the client, as part of the inspection sign-off process.
  • Technician information, including the inspector's name and any notes they enter while using the app.

We do not knowingly collect health information, financial information, or government identifiers (such as tax file numbers) through the app.

How we collect it

Information is collected directly through the technician's use of the app during an inspection — primarily through voice dictation, manual field entry, and (where enabled) photo capture and digital signatures. Clients do not interact with the app directly; their information is entered by the technician on their behalf, in the course of the inspection the client has engaged us to carry out.

Why we collect it

We collect this information to generate an accurate, structured pest inspection report for the client, allow the technician to review and finalise findings before the report is issued, maintain a record of past inspections for the technician's own reference, and support compliance with the relevant Australian Standards governing timber pest inspections (AS 3660.2 and AS 4349.3).

How your information is processed, including by AI

When a technician dictates their findings, the spoken transcript is sent to Anthropic's Claude API, a third-party artificial intelligence service, to convert the transcript into structured report fields. This is an automated processing step performed by software, not a human reviewer at Anthropic.

Some relevant facts about this step, based on Anthropic's published commercial API terms as at June 2026:

  • Data sent to the Claude API under commercial/API terms is not used to train Anthropic's AI models.
  • API request data is retained briefly by Anthropic for abuse-prevention and operational purposes, after which it is deleted.
  • Because Anthropic's infrastructure may process data outside Australia, using this feature involves an overseas disclosure of personal information to the United States or other jurisdictions in which Anthropic operates.

Where your information is stored

Inspection data, including drafts, saved reports, and any signatures captured, is currently stored locally on the technician's own device, within the app itself. We do not currently operate a central server or database that stores client information outside the technician's device, other than the brief processing described above. Finished reports are provided to the client as a PDF document; once issued, the client is responsible for their own copy.

Who we share information with

We share personal information with Anthropic, solely for the purpose described above, and with the client, who receives the finished inspection report containing their own property and contact information. We do not sell personal information, and we do not share it with any other third party for marketing or any other purpose.

How long we keep information

Technicians may retain saved inspection reports on their own device for as long as is useful for their records. We recommend technicians periodically review and delete reports that are no longer needed, particularly once a client relationship has concluded.

Security

Information captured through KORVA Inspect is stored on the technician's own device rather than transmitted to or stored on a central Korva-controlled server, other than the brief AI-processing step described above. Technicians are responsible for keeping their own device secure, since a lost or compromised device could expose stored inspection data.

Your rights

If you are a client and would like to access the personal information we hold about you, correct it, or ask a question about how it has been handled, you can contact us using the details below. We will respond to reasonable requests as promptly as we can.

If something goes wrong

If we become aware of a data breach involving personal information collected through KORVA Inspect that is likely to result in serious harm, we will take reasonable steps to contain it, assess the impact, and notify affected individuals and, where required, the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).

Children's information

KORVA Inspect is a professional tool used by licensed pest inspection technicians and is not directed at or designed for use by children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time, including as Korva's business structure, technical features, or applicable law changes. The "Last updated" date at the top of this policy reflects the most recent revision.
